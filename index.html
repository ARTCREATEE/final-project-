The games

What we played.

01

First-person shooter

Halo 3

LAN parties, split-screen, four controllers on one TV. You memorized every map. You knew exactly where the sniper rifle spawned on The Pit. You finish the fight.

02

Third-person shooter

Gears of War

Nobody had seen anything look like that before. The chainsaw on the gun was ridiculous and perfect. You and a friend played it in a single weekend and neither of you slept much.

03

Open world

Red Dead Redemption

You rode across that desert and felt genuinely alone in a good way. The ending hit different. You sat with it for a while after the credits rolled.

04

Skateboarding

Skate

The flick stick changed everything. Suddenly skating felt real — clumsy, physical, earned. You bailed a hundred times on the same line and kept going back.

05

Action RPG

Mass Effect 2

You built a crew. You learned their names, their problems, their histories. When the suicide mission came you were scared to lose them. Games hadn't done that before.

The hardware

The machine.

The console

White, boxy, louder than it needed to be. Released 2005. It ran hot, scratched discs if you moved it, and yellowed over time. You loved it anyway.

The controller

Wireless, comfortable, perfectly weighted. The four colored buttons became muscle memory. The thumbstick rubber wore off after a year. You didn't mind.

The red ring

Three red quadrants meant hardware failure. It happened to enough people that Microsoft extended the warranty to three years. You probably saw it at least once.

About

What this is.

The Xbox 360 was just a box. A loud, warm, slightly unreliable box that sat under your TV and made a lot of people very happy for a long time.

This site is a document of that era. The games, the hardware, the culture around it. No rankings. No scores. Just memory, written down before it fades completely.

If you were there, you already know what this feels like. If you weren’t — welcome. It was something.
